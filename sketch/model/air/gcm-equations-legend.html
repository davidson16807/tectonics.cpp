"All weather and climate result from three fundamental entities: motion (both horizontal and vertical), energy, and moisture. Without any of these, weather and climate cease to exist. We therefore seek to simplify the atmosphere to its most basic properties in order to isolate the fundamental equations that govern motion, energy, and moisture in the atmosphere. Seven equations accomplish this task."
-Rohli and Vega, "Climatology", Chapter 13
\(u\) wind velocity
\(\rho\) density
\(p\) pressure
\(T\) temperature
\(m\) specific humidity
\[ m=\frac{r}{r+1} \quad\text{or}\quad m=\frac{\epsilon e}{p} \]
\(P\) Precipitation
\(P\) Evaporation
\(f\) Coriolis force
\[ f=2\Omega\sin\varphi \]
\(F\) additional forcing
\(C_p\) constant pressure specific heat capacity
\(R_d\) dry air constant \((287\,Jkg^{-1}K^{-1})\)
\(R\) universal gas constant \((8.314\,J\,mol^{-1}K^{-1})\)
\(r\) mixing ratio
\(e\) vapor pressure
\[ e=e_0\exp\!\left( \frac{LM}{R} \left( \frac{1}{T_f} - \frac{1}{T_d} \right) \right) \]
\(e_0\) vapor pressure when \(T_d=T_f\) (611\(Pa\))
\(T_f\) Temperature at freezing
\(T_d\) Temperature at dew point
\(T_v\) Virtual temperature, temperature of dry air were its pressure and temperature equaled that of sampled air
\[ T_v= \frac{\left(1+\frac{r}{\epsilon}\right)} {1+r}T \]
\(L\) latent heat of condensation \((2.5008\cdot 10^6 \,Jkg^{-1})\) or sublimation \((2.83545\cdot 10^6 \,Jkg^{-1})\) if over ice surface
