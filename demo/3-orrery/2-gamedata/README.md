All orbital elements are as they would appear on January 1, 2000, at 12:00 Terrestrial Time (the "J2000 epoch"). 

Terrestrial Time (TT) is defined by international atomic time (TAI) so that 1977-01-01T00:00:32.184TT is exactly 1977-01-01T00:00:00.000TAI, or the instant at which TAI introduced corrections for gravitational time dilation. TAI is a weighted average of time kept by atomic clocks, originally in Coordinated Universal Time (UTC). UTC and TAI were synchronized at the start of 1958 and since then they have drifted apart largely due to Earth's slowing rotation. As of January 6, 2026, UTC is exactly 37 seconds behind TAI.

Data is taken from NASA JPL's "Horizons" Website where possible, specifically:
https://ssd.jpl.nasa.gov/planets/approx_pos.html for Semi-major axis, eccentricity, inclination, and longitude of ascending node for planets and
https://ssd.jpl.nasa.gov/sats/elem/ for all orbital elements of satellites of planets
https://ssd.jpl.nasa.gov/planets/phys_par.html for mass and radii

Masses were derived from:
https://ssd.jpl.nasa.gov/astro_par.html
assuming a gravitational constant of 6.6743015e-11 m³kg⁻¹s⁻² as per the CRC chemical property handbook
