* once light is reflected or emitted, this model assumes all directionality is lost
* we might approximate direction of reflected/emitted light as "straight up", 
  which gives a minimum estimate of absorption
* the alternate implementation is to create a dynamic surrogate f:L→F that for a direction of incoming light L 
  estimates the fraction F that reflects as the integration over the sphere of all possible directions of reflection

aspects that completely determine the climate model:
* light exposures
* grid properties (surface area, surface normal)
* world radius, surface gravity
* rock albedo, height
* sea albedo, sea composition, depth
* air composition, scale height

aspects of climate that affect other components:
* temperature
* rainfall
* snowfall
